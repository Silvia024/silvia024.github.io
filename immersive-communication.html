Immersive Communications

Virtual Reality (VR) systems are expected to become widespread in the near future, with applications not limited to entertainment but extending to e-healthcare, e-education, and many others. VR communications involve omnidirectional (360° or spherical) videos that provide an immersive sensation to the viewer, who is virtually placed at the centre of a sphere and can navigate the scene with 3-Degrees of Freedom (3-DoF) or, in more advanced 6-DoF systems, move freely through the environment.

The novel geometry of this content, the high spatio-temporal resolution required, and the stringent low-latency constraints raise new challenges across the entire end-to-end multimedia chain — from signal acquisition and representation to compression, delivery, and rendering. Our research is focused on optimising the media delivery of VR content over band-limited networks and developing personalised, user-, content- and application-dependent adaptive streaming strategies.
